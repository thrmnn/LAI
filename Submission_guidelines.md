Submission Requirements Report: Nature Cities

This report details the technical and editorial requirements for submitting manuscripts to Nature Cities. As a Nature Research journal, Nature Cities maintains rigorous standards for transparency and reproducibility. This document serves as a guide for authors to ensure their submissions align with institutional policies and technical specifications.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


1. Initial Submission Formatting Standards

To streamline the editorial process, Nature Cities allows for flexible formatting during the initial submission stage, provided the manuscript is presented in a manner suitable for rigorous editorial assessment.

* Format Flexibility: No specific house styling or layout is required for the first stage of submission.
* Accepted File Formats: Manuscripts must be submitted as PDF, Microsoft Word, or TeX/LaTeX files.
* LaTeX Requirements: Authors utilizing TeX/LaTeX are required to submit a compiled PDF for the review process.
* Post-Acceptance Requirements: Strict adherence to the journal’s formatting standards for text and imagery is mandatory only upon formal acceptance of the manuscript.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


2. Required Submission Components

A complete submission must include a manuscript file, a cover letter, and—where applicable—supplementary information.

2.1 Manuscript File Requirements

The manuscript file is the core of the submission and must include the following elements. Note for Double-Blind Peer Review: If you opt for double-blind review, the manuscript file itself must be completely anonymized; all author names, affiliations, and contact details must be removed from this file and included only in the cover letter.

* Names and Affiliations: Provide the full names and institutional affiliations for all co-authors. The primary affiliation for each author must be the institution where the majority of their work was conducted. If an author's location has changed since the study's completion, a current address may also be listed.
* Methods: This section must provide a detailed description of the study. It must contain sufficient information on methods and materials to enable replication of the study by a fellow expert. While the section should be concise, it must not compromise on the detail necessary for interpretation and reproducibility.
  * Protocols: The journal strongly encourages authors to leverage Protocols.io—an open resource maintained by Nature Research—to deposit step-by-step protocols. Once published, these protocols will be linked directly to the online Methods section.
* References: A comprehensive and accurate reference list is mandatory.
* Extended Data (Optional): Authors may include up to ten Extended Data figures or tables. These are essential display items that provide critical background to the main paper but are excluded from the primary layout due to space constraints or because they are of interest primarily to specialists.

2.2 Cover Letter Content

The cover letter is a confidential document seen only by the editorial team; it is not shared with peer reviewers. It should be used to contextualize the work and demonstrate its relevance to the diverse readership of Nature Cities.

Required Information Checklist:

* [ ] A clear explanation of the work’s significance and its suitability for the journal's interdisciplinary audience.
* [ ] Disclosure of any related manuscripts currently under consideration or in press at other outlets.
* [ ] Details regarding any prior discussions held with a Nature Cities editor.
* [ ] For Double-Blind Review: Full names, affiliations, and contact information for all authors.

Optional Information:

* [ ] Recommended reviewers: Names and institutional affiliations of qualified experts.
* [ ] Reviewer exclusions: Names of individuals who should not review the manuscript, accompanied by a brief justification.

2.3 Supplementary Information

Supplementary Information consists of data or display items that are not central to the description of the main findings but are necessary to properly understand and replicate the study.

* Submission Standard: Only information directly relevant to the paper’s conclusions should be included.
* Review Access: These files are considered integral to the evaluation process and will be sent to peer reviewers alongside the manuscript.

3. Authorship and AI Usage Policies

3.1 Criteria for Authorship

Authorship implies both significant contribution and personal accountability for the published work. Affiliations must accurately reflect where the research was performed.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


4. The Submission and Review Workflow

4.1 Pre-submission Checklist: What You Need to Know

Before initiating a submission, authors must consult the following:

1. Aims and Scope: Confirm the manuscript aligns with the journal’s editorial focus.
2. Content Types: Verify that your specific manuscript format is accepted.
3. Editorial Policies: Review all governing Nature Research policies.
4. Publishing Options and Costs: Understand the available publishing models and associated Article Processing Charges (APCs).
5. Presubmission Enquiries: Determine if an informal enquiry is appropriate for your work.
6. Preprint and "In Review" Policies: Review the rules regarding the use of preprint servers and the "In Review" service.

4.2 Online Submission Steps

Authors must use the online manuscript tracking system. The process involves:

* Account Management: Users must log in or register for a new account.
* Data Entry and Uploading: Follow system prompts to input metadata and upload the manuscript, cover letter, and supplementary files.

4.3 Editorial and Peer Review Stages

Following submission, the editorial team performs an initial assessment. If the manuscript proceeds to peer review, all authors will receive an email requiring them to read and sign the journal’s Editorial Policies.

4.4 Post-Acceptance Requirements

Manuscripts that pass peer review reach the "Acceptance in Principle" stage. Before final acceptance:

* Final Formatting: Authors must apply the strict house style to all text and display items.
* ORCID iD: The corresponding author is required to link their Open Researcher and Contributor Identifier (ORCID) to the manuscript tracking system account.
